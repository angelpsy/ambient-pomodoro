# Ambient Pomodoro — Vision

## Product Summary
Ambient Pomodoro is a **low-distraction focus timer** that gently informs the user about time passing, without demanding action or breaking concentration.

The app exists to support **awareness**, not to enforce productivity.

---

## Problem Statement
Most Pomodoro timers:
- demand immediate reaction
- interrupt deep focus
- impose rigid work/rest cycles
- create pressure and obligation

This works for routine tasks, but actively harms deep or prolonged focus.

---

## Vision
Ambient Pomodoro provides:
- calm time awareness
- predictable signals
- full user control
- zero urgency

The user is always informed, never rushed.

---

## Core Idea
> The timer continues.
> The signal informs.
> The user decides.

No forced breaks. No penalties. No productivity pressure.

---

## Target Use Case
- working alone
- at home
- without headphones
- during long focus sessions
- where interruptions must stay minimal

---

## What the Product Is
- a single, continuous focus timer
- a reminder that a break can be taken
- an ambient signal in the background
- a tool for self-regulation, not discipline

---

## What the Product Is Not
- a productivity coach
- a habit builder
- a task manager
- a gamified system
- a strict Pomodoro enforcer

---

## Success Criteria
The product is successful if:
- users do not feel interrupted
- users do not feel rushed
- users do not feel loss of control
- users remain aware of time passing
- ignoring the app feels safe

---

## Design Philosophy
- **Calm** over efficiency
- **Awareness** over enforcement
- **Predictability** over motivation
- **Control** over automation

---

## Long-Term Direction (Non-Commitment)
Potential future directions may include:
- configurable intervals
- multiple timers
- custom sounds

These are not commitments and do not affect the current scope.

---

## Vision Stability
This document:
- should rarely change
- must guide all other documents
- overrides local optimizations or conveniences
